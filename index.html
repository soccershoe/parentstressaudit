The Paternal Schedule Intensity ($\mathbf{PSI_{Score}}$)

The **PSI Score** quantifies your stress level using eight weighted factors covering physical, time, emotional, and financial burdens.

$$\mathbf{PSI_{Score}} = \mathbf{F_S} + \mathbf{F_C} + \mathbf{F_O} + \mathbf{F_J} + \mathbf{F_D} + \mathbf{F_K} + \mathbf{F_G} + \mathbf{F_E}$$

Fₓ (Sleep Debt Factor): $$\max(0, 7.5 - \text{SleepHours}) \times 10$$ Penalizes hours below 7.5; lack of sleep compounds all other stressors (Sleep Foundation).

Fᴄ (Commute Strain Factor): $$\max\left(0, \frac{\text{TotalWeeklyCommuteMinutes} - \text{Days} \times 60}{\text{Days} \times 60}\right) \times 20$$ Long commutes (over 60 min RT/day) are strongly linked to increased stress, anxiety, and loss of family time (ADP/Urban Institute).

Fᴏ (Obligation Load Factor): $$\max(0, \text{ObligationHours} - 3) \times 4$$ Penalizes time spent weekly on personal, non-core commitments (hobbies, volunteering, side gigs) above a 3-hour baseline.

Fᴊ (Job Load Factor): $$\text{JobStressMultiplier} \times 5$$ Captures stress from high-stakes responsibility (Manager Multiplier=1, Executive Multiplier=2). Managers report higher burnout rates than ICs (Gallup Report).

Fᴅ (Domestic Load Factor): $$\max\left(0, \frac{\text{Chore Share}}{50} - 1\right) \times 15$$ Quantifies stress from unequal domestic labor (including the mental load). Unequal division is a major driver of psychological distress (USC Dornsife Research).

Fᴋ (Child Load Factor): $$\max(0, \text{NumKids} - 1) \times 5$$ Adds a multiplier for each child after the first, reflecting the exponential increase in logistical demands from multiple children (Source: Ohio State University Study).

Fɢ (Financial Strain Factor): $$\text{FinancialWorryScore}$$ Directly uses the self-reported score (1-15) for financial stress, which is a common source of chronic, diffuse stress.

Fᴇ (Energy Mismatch Factor): $$\text{MismatchPenalty}$$ A penalty applied when an **Introvert** is in a **High-Interaction** work role (like management), leading to profound emotional and mental drain.

Interpretation Scale:

  • **0 - 10:** Low Intensity (Exceptional balance).
  • **11 - 30:** Average Intensity (Typical modern working parent).
  • **31 - 50:** High Intensity (Significant stress, lifestyle adjustments needed).
  • **51+:** Extreme Intensity (Burnout imminent/present - Critical change required).

If your score is above 30, consider these external resources for finding balance:

  • **Parental Burnout Support:** Strategies for coping with chronic exhaustion and detachment (Psychology Today).
  • **Sleep and Recovery:** Essential steps to maximize the quality of the sleep you do get (CDC's Tips for Better Sleep).
  • **Boundary Setting:** Actionable strategies for protecting your time and energy from work and outside demands (Mayo Clinic).
  • **Addressing the Mental Load:** Resources for having constructive conversations about rebalancing domestic planning and mental labor with your partner.
